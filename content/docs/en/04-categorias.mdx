# Custom categories

**Categories** let you classify your transactions for later analysis. They're fully customizable: you define the name, color, and icon of each one.

---

## Expense vs income categories

Each category belongs to one type: **expense** or **income**. There's no "transfer" category — transfers between accounts aren't categorized.

This means:

- Expense-type categories only appear when recording an expense.
- Income-type categories only appear when recording income.
- In reports, you can filter by type and see the corresponding categories.

---

## Creating a category

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Open Categories">
    From the bottom bar, tap **More**, then **Categories**.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Tap the + button">
    The FAB opens the new category form.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Fill in the form">
    - **Name**: e.g., "Groceries", "Restaurants", "Salary".
    - **Type**: expense or income.
    - **Icon**: choose from the available icon palette (based on Material Icons).
    - **Color**: any color from the system palette.
  </Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Save">
    The category is immediately available when recording transactions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="No default categories">
  The app doesn't come with pre-loaded categories — you start from scratch. This is intentional: everyone has different spending habits. Create the ones you'll actually use so that reports are meaningful.
</Callout>

## Common category ideas

**Expenses:**

- Housing (rent, HOA, utilities)
- Groceries (supermarket, farmers market)
- Restaurants & cafés
- Transportation (gas, public transit, taxis)
- Health (pharmacy, doctor, insurance)
- Entertainment (streaming, movies, outings)
- Education
- Clothing
- Technology

**Income:**

- Salary
- Freelance / consulting
- Rental income
- Dividends / returns
- Item sales

---

## Editing a category

Tap any category in the list. The edit form lets you change name, type, color, and icon.

<Callout type="warning" title="Changing the type may hide categories in the form">
  If you change a category from "expense" to "income", all expense transactions that used it still exist with that category — but you won't see it available when creating new expenses. This is an edge case; if you need to reorganize, it's better to create new categories.
</Callout>

---

## Deleting a category

Swipe the category to the left to delete it.

<Callout type="danger" title="Transactions are not deleted">
  When you delete a category, the transactions that had it assigned **are not deleted** — they just lose their category. You'll still see them in the history but they won't appear in the category breakdown in reports.
</Callout>

---

## How categories are used in reports

Categories are the foundation of financial analysis in reports:

- **Pie chart**: distribution of expenses (or income) by category in the selected period.
- **Ranking**: top 5 most-spent categories.
- **Filter**: you can view the history of a single category from the Transactions screen.

The more consistently you categorize your transactions, the more useful reports become. A transaction without a category doesn't appear in charts.

---

## Limits

- No limit on the number of categories.
- No subcategories — the hierarchy is flat.
- Names don't need to be unique per type — you can have "Miscellaneous" in expenses and "Miscellaneous" in income; they're separate entities.
