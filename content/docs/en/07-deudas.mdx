# Debts & loans

The **Debts** section lets you track money you lent or borrowed, with partial payment tracking and an editable history.

To access: **More > Debts**.

---

## Debt types

Mis Gastos distinguishes two directions:

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **They owe me** (`theyOweMe`) | You lent money; someone owes it to you. |
| **I owe** (`iOwe`) | Someone lent you money; you owe it to them. |

---

## Screen tabs

The screen has four tabs:

1. **By person**: groups all active debts by counterparty (person or entity name).
2. **They owe me**: list of active "They owe me" debts.
3. **I owe**: list of active "I owe" debts.
4. **Paid**: fully settled debts.

---

## Creating a debt

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Tap the + button">
    The FAB on the Debts screen opens the form.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Fill in the form">
    - **Counterparty**: name of the person or entity (e.g., "John", "Bank of America").
    - **Type**: whether you owe or are owed.
    - **Currency**: the currency the debt is denominated in.
    - **Original amount**: the total amount of the debt.
    - **Due date** (optional): if the debt has a deadline.
    - **Note** (optional): additional context.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Save">
    The debt appears in the corresponding list with the outstanding amount equal to the original amount.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Recording a partial payment

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Open the debt">
    Tap the debt in the list to see its detail.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Tap Record payment">
    The payment button opens the partial payment form.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Fill in the payment">
    - **Amount**: how much was paid in this installment. Can be any value up to the outstanding amount.
    - **Date**: the payment date.
    - **Account** (optional): if you want to link the payment to an app account so it also records the transaction.
    - **Note** (optional).
  </Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Save">
    The outstanding amount on the debt decreases by the payment amount. If the total paid reaches the original amount, the debt automatically moves to **Paid**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Payments linked to an account">
  If you select an account when recording the payment, the app automatically creates a transaction in that account (income or expense depending on the debt direction). This is the cleanest way to keep the account balance in sync with the payment.
</Callout>

---

## Summary panel

At the top of the Debts screen there's a summary strip showing:

- **Total owed to you**: sum of all active "They owe me" debts, in base currency.
- **Total you owe**: sum of all active "I owe" debts, in base currency.

Debts in currencies other than the base are converted using the configured rates.

---

## Overdue debts

If you configured a due date and the debt is still active after that date, it appears marked as **overdue** in the list.

<Callout type="warning" title="It's just a visual indicator">
  The "overdue" mark is informational — the app doesn't automatically block or charge anything. It's a visual reminder that the deadline has passed.
</Callout>

---

## Editing or deleting a debt

Tap the debt to see editing options. You can change all fields from the original form.

Deleting a debt also deletes its payment history. Payments that generated account transactions **are not deleted** — they remain as independent records.

---

## Payment history

The detail view of each debt shows the complete list of recorded payments: date, amount, and note. You can edit or delete each payment individually.

<Callout type="info" title="Editing a payment recalculates the outstanding amount">
  If you modify the amount of a payment, the debt's outstanding amount recalculates automatically. Same if you delete a payment.
</Callout>

---

## Complete example

Scenario: you lent $500 to Maria on January 1st. She made two partial payments.

1. Create a "They owe me" debt, counterparty "Maria", original amount $500.
2. On January 15th, Maria pays $200 → record a payment of $200. Outstanding: $300.
3. On February 1st, Maria pays the rest → record a payment of $300. Outstanding: $0. The debt moves to "Paid".

In the "By person" tab you'll see both debts grouped under "Maria" while they're active.
