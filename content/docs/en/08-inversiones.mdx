# Investments & portfolio

The **Investments** section lets you track financial assets outside of regular accounts: stocks, cryptocurrencies, bonds, fixed deposits, ETFs, funds, and more.

To access: **More > Investments**.

---

## Supported investment types

| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Stocks** | Apple, Google, local equities |
| **Cryptocurrencies** | Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT |
| **Bonds** | Government bonds, corporate bonds |
| **ETF** | SPY, QQQ, sector ETFs |
| **Fund** | Mutual funds, hedge funds |
| **Real estate** | Properties, land |
| **Fixed deposit** | Savings deposits, time deposits |
| **Other** | Any unlisted asset |

---

## Creating an investment

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Open Investments">
    From **More > Investments**, tap the FAB.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Fill in the base data">
    - **Name**: identifier for the investment (e.g., "BTC", "AAPL", "NYC Apartment").
    - **Type**: one of the eight available types.
    - **Currency**: the currency it's denominated in.
    - **Current value**: the current market value. This field is updated manually whenever you want to reflect a price change.
    - **Color and icon**: for visual identification.
    - **Note** (optional).
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Save">
    The investment appears in the portfolio list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Investment movements

Each investment can have four types of movements:

| Type | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Deposit** | You bought or deposited money into this investment. |
| **Withdrawal** | You sold or withdrew money from this investment. |
| **Dividend / Return** | You received a dividend or interest payment. |
| **Update value** | You update the current market value with no money movement. |

To record a movement, open the investment and tap **+ Movement**.

<Callout type="info" title="Deposits and withdrawals can link to an account">
  When recording a deposit or withdrawal, you can select an app account. If you do, a transaction is automatically created in that account (expense for a deposit, income for a withdrawal) so the account balance also stays updated.
</Callout>

---

## ROI calculation

Return on investment (ROI) is calculated automatically from movements:

```
Gain = Current value + Total withdrawn + Total dividends − Total deposited
ROI% = (Gain / Total deposited) × 100
```

**Example:**

- You deposited $1,000 into AAPL stock.
- You received $20 in dividends.
- Current value of the shares is $1,150.
- ROI = (1,150 + 20 − 1,000) / 1,000 × 100 = **17%**

<Callout type="tip" title="ROI is based on total deposited, not net invested">
  If you deposited $1,000 and later withdrew $200, the base is still $1,000 (total deposited), not $800 (net invested). This may differ from other ROI calculators.
</Callout>

## Updating the market value

Investments don't update themselves — the app has no connection to price services. To update the value:

1. Open the investment.
2. Tap **+ Movement**.
3. Choose type **Update value**.
4. Enter the new total market value.

This movement doesn't create any account transaction — it just updates the "current value" field of the investment.

---

## Portfolio view

The main Investments screen shows:

- List of all investments with current value and ROI.
- Portfolio total in base currency (summing all investments converted).

The portfolio total is also shown on the home dashboard as part of net worth if you have investments loaded.

---

## Deleting an investment

Tap the investment and find the delete option.

<Callout type="danger" title="Deleting removes all investment movements">
  If the investment had movements linked to accounts, the account transactions **are not deleted** — they remain as independent records. Only the investment and its internal history are deleted.
</Callout>

---

## Limitations

- No real-time prices — everything is manual.
- Current value is a single number (no automatic price history). The "Update value" movement history serves as a manual price record.
- No support for multiple positions (lots at different prices) — each investment is a single position.
