# Account management

**Accounts** are the foundation of Mis Gastos. Every payment method you use — debit card, digital wallet, bank account, cash — lives as a separate account with its own balance, color, and currency.

---

## Account types

The app supports five types:

| Type | When to use it |
|---|---|
| **Card** | Debit, credit, or prepaid |
| **Wallet** | Digital (Mercado Pago, PayPal, etc.) |
| **Bank** | Checking or savings account |
| **Cash** | Physical money you carry |
| **Other** | Any method not listed above |

The type doesn't affect functionality — it's purely visual to identify the account at a glance.

---

## Creating an account

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Open the Accounts section">
    Tap **Accounts** in the bottom navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Tap the + button">
    The FAB (floating action button) in the bottom right opens the new account form.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Fill in the fields">
    - **Name**: what you want to call the account (e.g., "Visa Santander").
    - **Type**: choose from the five available types.
    - **Currency**: the currency this account operates in.
    - **Initial balance**: the balance the account has when you create it. Use 0 if you're starting fresh or will load history later.
    - **Color**: any color from the palette; helps distinguish accounts at a glance.
    - **Icon**: pick the icon that best represents the account.
  </Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Save">
    Tap **Save**. The account appears instantly in the accounts list and on the home dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Initial balance">
  If you already have previous transactions you won't be entering, use the current real balance as the initial balance. If you're going to load the full history, start at 0 and the balance will be calculated from your transactions.
</Callout>

---

## How the balance is calculated

The balance shown for each account is dynamic. The app calculates it as:

```
Current balance = Initial balance + Σ(income) − Σ(expenses) ± Σ(transfers)
```

You never store the balance directly — it's always derived from your transactions. This means if you correct or delete a transaction, the balance updates instantly.

---

## Editing an account

Tap any account in the list to open it. From the detail view you can:

- Change name, type, color, or icon.
- Modify the initial balance.
- View the transaction history filtered to that account.

<Callout type="warning" title="You can't change the currency of an account with transactions">
  If an account already has recorded transactions, changing its currency would distort all historical amounts. If you need to make the change, create a new account and transfer the balance.
</Callout>

---

## Deleting an account

Swipe the account to the left in the list to reveal the delete button. The app asks for confirmation.

<Callout type="danger" title="Deleting removes all associated transactions">
  When you delete an account, all its transactions **are deleted too**. If you just want to hide the account, there's currently no "archive" option — consider renaming it "Inactive" as a workaround.
</Callout>

---

## Accounts in multiple currencies

You can have accounts in different currencies. For example:

- Account "USD Cash" → USD currency
- Account "ARS Account" → ARS currency
- Account "Crypto ETH" → ETH currency

The **total net worth** on the home dashboard converts all balances to the base currency using the rates you've configured. See [Currencies & exchange rates](/en/docs/05-monedas).

---

## Limits and considerations

- There's no limit on the number of accounts.
- One account can't have two different currencies — for that, create two separate accounts.
- The app doesn't block recording expenses that would take a balance negative — it just shows it visually below zero.
